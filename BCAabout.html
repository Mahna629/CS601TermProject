Black Creek Acres was started in Churchville, New York by Bob Maysick after retiring from Kodak in 1980. He was soon joined in this venture by his son Bill.

Beginning in 1983, Black Creek Acres operated primarily as a dairy farm, selling milk through the Upstate Farms cooperative.

In 2015, dairy operations ceased. Bill's son Ben, who lives in the on-sight farmhouse, currently handles much of the farm management, which now harvests and sells a variety of crops, such as hay, wheat, and non-GMO soy beans and corn.

In the future, they are looking to start producing maple syrup and honey as well.
